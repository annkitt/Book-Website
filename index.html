Fiction Books

Sin Eater by Megan Campisi

"Sin Eater" is a thought-provoking novel that examines the nature of sin, the power of redemption, and the lengths one will go to protect the ones they love. With its blend of supernatural elements, suspenseful plot twists, and deep emotional resonance, this novel promises an enthralling reading experience for fans of dark fantasy and atmospheric storytelling.

The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald

"The Great Gatsby" is a poignant portrayal of the illusions and disillusionments of the American Dream, as well as a critique of the excessive materialism and moral decay of the era. Fitzgerald's evocative prose, vivid descriptions, and complex characters make it a timeless exploration of love, identity, and the corrupting influence of wealth and societal expectations.

1984 by George orwell

The story culminates in a chilling and tragic ending that showcases the complete control and domination of the Party over its citizens. "1984" continues to be a powerful and thought-provoking work that serves as a cautionary tale about the dangers of authoritarianism and the importance of preserving individual liberty.

Non-Fiction Books

Atmoic Habits by James Clear

"Atomic Habits" provides readers with a roadmap for creating lasting positive change in their personal and professional lives. Clear's engaging writing style, combined with practical examples and actionable advice, make this book a valuable resource for anyone seeking to improve their habits and achieve long-term success.

Quiet by Susan Cain

"Quiet" is a transformative book that empowers introverts to embrace their natural tendencies, and it encourages a shift in societal perceptions and values to foster a more inclusive and understanding world for all personality types. She explains how introverts thrive in quieter, more reflective environments and how they often possess deep thinking, creativity, and empathy.

Educated by Tara Westover

"Educated" is a deeply personal and inspiring memoir that offers a poignant reflection on the importance of education, the complexities of family relationships, and the pursuit of self-discovery. It is a testament to the resilience of the human spirit and the transformative power of education in breaking free from the constraints of one's past.
